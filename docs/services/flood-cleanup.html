Flood Cleanup in Tulsa — Oklahoma Storm Response Done Right

IICRC Certified | 24/7 Emergency Response | All Insurance Accepted

Flood cleanup is not the same job as a burst pipe. Oklahoma's severe storm season pushes floodwater through neighborhoods, basements, and crawl spaces — carrying soil, debris, and contaminants that require a different response than clean-water extraction. Tulsa sits in Green Country, one of the state's most storm-active corridors, and spring flood events can overwhelm drainage systems in minutes. When it happens, the scope of damage depends entirely on how fast the response is.

When You Need Flood Cleanup

Flood cleanup is needed any time water enters your home from outside — storm runoff, overflowing creeks, sewer backup during heavy rain, or rising water that finds its way through foundation cracks and windows. Unlike a burst pipe (clean water), flood intrusion is typically Category 2 or Category 3 contaminated water. This classification means standard wet-dry vacuuming and fan-drying is not enough — contaminated water requires proper extraction protocols, antimicrobial treatment, and in many cases, removal of saturated porous materials. Attempting to dry flood-affected rooms without proper treatment leaves bacterial contamination behind that can cause health issues and insurance complications.

How Flood Cleanup Works

Step 1 — Safety Assessment: We confirm structural safety, identify electrical hazards, and determine the water category before anyone enters the affected area.

Step 2 — Water Extraction: Truck-mounted pumps and portable extractors remove standing water. We document water levels and extent before and during removal.

Step 3 — Material Removal: Saturated flooring, drywall, and insulation that cannot be salvaged is removed. Leaving wet porous materials in place creates mold problems that appear weeks after the event.

Step 4 — Antimicrobial Treatment: All affected surfaces — subfloor, framing, concrete — are treated with EPA-registered antimicrobial solutions before structural drying begins.

Step 5 — Structural Drying: Industrial air movers and commercial dehumidifiers run continuously until daily moisture readings confirm complete drying throughout the structure.

What Every Flood Cleanup Job Includes

  • Water category assessment (Category 1/2/3) before any work begins
  • Full standing water extraction with truck-mounted equipment
  • Removal of non-salvageable flooring, drywall, and insulation
  • EPA-registered antimicrobial treatment of all affected surfaces
  • Industrial drying equipment — air movers and commercial dehumidifiers
  • Daily moisture readings with documented drying progress
  • Complete insurance documentation — photos, moisture logs, material inventory
  • Written scope of work and reconstruction plan

What Does Flood Cleanup Cost in Tulsa?

Flood cleanup runs $1,500–$8,000+ depending on the area affected, the water category, and how much material requires removal. A contained basement event with clean-ish water is on the lower end. A multi-room Category 3 flood with saturated framing and flooring replacement runs considerably higher. Most standard homeowner's policies cover sudden water intrusion from storms — wind-driven rain, roof damage, storm-driven overflow. Rising floodwater requires a separate NFIP flood policy. We help you determine which policy applies and handle documentation for your claim.

Questions About Flood Cleanup

Is flood cleanup different from regular water damage restoration?

Yes. Floodwater is typically contaminated and classified as Category 2 or Category 3 water. This requires different protocols — proper PPE during extraction, antimicrobial treatment of all affected surfaces, and removal of porous materials that can't be fully decontaminated. Standard drying-only approaches on flood events leave contamination behind.

Does homeowner's insurance cover flooding in Tulsa?

Standard homeowner's policies cover water damage from storms — wind-driven rain, roof intrusion, broken windows. They do NOT cover rising ground-level floodwater, which requires a separate NFIP (National Flood Insurance Program) policy. We help identify which policy applies and document the claim appropriately.

How long does flood cleanup take?

Initial extraction and material removal typically takes 1–2 days. Structural drying runs 3–5 days continuously until moisture readings confirm complete drying. Reconstruction scope determines the timeline after that — we provide a written estimate before any phase begins.

Can I stay in my home during flood cleanup?

It depends on the scope. Minor events confined to a basement or single room often allow normal occupancy. Larger events with Category 3 water or significant material removal may require temporary relocation during the active work phase. We'll tell you honestly what's required during the initial assessment.
