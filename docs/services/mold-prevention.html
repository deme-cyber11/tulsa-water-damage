IICRC Certified | 24/7 Emergency Response | All Insurance Accepted

Oklahoma's humid subtropical climate makes mold one of the most serious long-term consequences of water damage in Tulsa homes. Mold spores are always present in our environment â€” all they need to begin colonizing is moisture and an organic surface. After any water event, that window opens fast. Why Oklahoma Homes Are High Risk

Tulsa's humidity regularly exceeds 70% during spring and summer months. When water-damaged materials are left to dry on their own â€” or dried improperly â€” interior relative humidity rises and creates ideal conditions for mold growth within wall cavities, under flooring, and in HVAC systems. Professional drying equipment keeps indoor humidity controlled throughout the process.

Our Mold Prevention Protocol

  • Application of EPA-registered antimicrobial treatment to all wet surfaces during the initial extraction phase
  • Controlled drying environment maintained with commercial dehumidifiers
  • Removal of non-salvageable organic materials (drywall, insulation, carpet) before they can become mold substrates
  • Daily moisture monitoring to confirm drying progress
  • Final inspection with moisture meter readings at or below IICRC-recommended baselines

When Mold Has Already Established

If you're dealing with existing mold growth after a past water event, we provide full IICRC S520-protocol mold remediation. This includes source control, containment, air filtration with HEPA units, removal of contaminated materials, and surface treatment. We do not simply paint over or mask mold â€” we remove it properly.

Signs of Hidden Mold After Water Damage

  • Musty odor that won't resolve after drying
  • Discoloration appearing on walls or ceilings weeks after the water event
  • Increased allergy or respiratory symptoms in family members
  • Warped or bubbling wall surfaces indicating hidden moisture
