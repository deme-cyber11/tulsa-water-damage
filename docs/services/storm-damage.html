Storm Damage Restoration in Tulsa — From First Response to Finished Rebuild

IICRC Certified | Insurance Documentation | 24/7 Emergency Response

Oklahoma tornado season and severe thunderstorm activity make Tulsa homeowners uniquely vulnerable to storm-driven water damage. Wind-driven rain, roof intrusion, hail damage, and flash flooding can combine in a single storm event — creating multiple water entry points that standard extraction misses if the full scope isn't assessed at the start. Storm damage restoration is a whole-home response, not just a wet-carpet call.

When You Need Storm Damage Restoration

After any significant Oklahoma storm, the signs that require immediate professional response: water stains spreading on ceilings, damp walls that weren't damp before, musty odor appearing within hours of a storm, pooled water in basements or crawl spaces, or visible damage to roofing or windows that allowed water entry. The danger with storm damage is that water enters at elevation — through roofs and windows — and then moves downward through wall cavities and floor systems, saturating hidden areas that won't show visible signs for days. What looks like a contained ceiling stain is often wet insulation, saturated framing, and water that has tracked through multiple floors.

How Storm Damage Restoration Works

Step 1 — Emergency Stabilization: If the storm event is recent or ongoing, we tarp damaged roof areas and board windows to stop additional water entry before extraction begins.

Step 2 — Full-Structure Assessment: Moisture meters and infrared cameras map all affected areas — including walls, ceilings, and floors that don't show visible damage but are holding water.

Step 3 — Water Extraction: All standing water is removed with truck-mounted and portable extraction equipment. Sewage backup related to storm events uses Category 3 protocols.

Step 4 — Material Removal: Saturated insulation, drywall, and flooring that cannot be dried in place is removed to expose the framing and allow complete structural drying.

Step 5 — Structural Drying and Rebuild: Industrial drying equipment runs until documented clearance. Reconstruction — drywall, insulation, flooring, paint — completes the job with one company start to finish.

What Every Storm Damage Restoration Job Includes

  • Water extraction — all categories of storm water addressed
  • Removal of non-salvageable materials

  • Mold prevention treatment applied to all affected areas

What Does Storm Damage Restoration Cost in Tulsa?

Storm damage restoration ranges from $1,500 for a contained ceiling leak to $30,000+ for a whole-home event involving tornado damage, major flooding, and full reconstruction. The variables are the entry points (one roof breach vs. multiple), the areas affected, the categories of water involved, and the scope of required rebuild. Most standard homeowner's policies cover sudden storm damage — wind-driven rain, hail, roof damage, and storm-driven water intrusion. We document everything your adjuster needs and can communicate directly with your carrier.

Questions About Storm Damage Restoration

What should I do immediately after a storm damages my home?

Call us first. While waiting for response, turn off electricity to areas with water if it's safe to do so. Don't run fans in areas with potential sewage contamination. Don't attempt to extract water from areas with structural damage until we assess stability. Document with your phone camera if you can safely do so.

Does homeowner's insurance cover tornado and storm damage?

Yes. Standard homeowner's policies cover wind damage, storm-driven rain, and hail damage — this is the most common type of water damage insurance claim in Oklahoma. We document the damage and water intrusion path to support maximum coverage. Rising flood water requires a separate NFIP policy.

My ceiling has a water stain but it seems dry — do I need restoration?

A dry-looking stain doesn't mean the structure is dry. Water travels through building materials before appearing on surfaces, and it dries from the outside in. A ceiling stain often means wet insulation and framing above it. We use moisture meters to confirm whether the structure is actually dry or just surface-dry.

Can you handle both the restoration and the rebuild?

Yes. We handle the full restoration process through reconstruction — drywall, flooring, insulation, paint. You don't need to find a separate contractor after the restoration phase. One point of contact from first call to finished home.

Oklahoma Storm Season and Tulsa Water Damage — What Homeowners Face

Tulsa sits in one of the most storm-active corridors in the United States. Spring severe weather season — roughly March through June — brings tornado watches, supercell thunderstorms, golf ball-sized hail, and rainfall events that can drop three inches in under an hour. The combination of high winds, large hail that damages roofing, and intense rainfall creates conditions where even a structurally sound home can take on significant water damage in a single weather event.

The pattern Tulsa Water Damage Pros sees repeatedly after major storm events: a homeowner notices a water stain on a ceiling the morning after a storm and assumes it's minor. They wait a few days to see if it's a problem. By the time they call, the insulation above the ceiling is thoroughly saturated, water has tracked down wall cavities, and the subfloor in an adjacent room is wet. What would have been a fast extraction and drying job has become a multi-room remediation with insulation removal and drywall replacement. The difference in cost and disruption is significant — and entirely preventable with a fast response call.

After any significant storm event in Tulsa — tornado, straight-line winds, major hail, or heavy rain — we recommend a moisture inspection even if no visible damage is apparent. We use infrared cameras and moisture meters to confirm whether water has entered the structure. If it has, we address it before the 24–48 hour mold window closes.
