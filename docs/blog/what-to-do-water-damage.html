What to Do in the First Hour of a Water Damage Emergency

February 2025 · 6 min read · By Tulsa Water Damage Pros

A pipe bursts. Water pours from your ceiling. Your basement has two inches of standing water. Your hands are shaking and you don't know what to do first.

We get calls like this every day in Tulsa. In those first moments, what you do — or don't do — can mean the difference between a $3,000 cleanup and a $25,000 restoration project. Here's exactly what to do, in order.

First things first: Call (918) 723-2096 immediately. You can do everything else on this list while our crew is on the way.

Step 1: Call a Water Damage Restoration Company (Not a Plumber)

Most people's instinct is to call a plumber. That's wrong. A plumber can fix the broken pipe — but they can't extract water, dry your structure, or prevent mold. By the time they arrive, assess, fix the leak, and leave, you've lost hours of critical drying time.

Call a 24/7 water damage restoration company like Tulsa Water Damage Pros first. We can diagnose the water source ourselves and coordinate with a plumber if needed, while simultaneously beginning water extraction. Call (918) 723-2096 now.

Step 2: Shut Off the Water Source

If the water is coming from a broken pipe, shut off your home's main water supply immediately. The main shutoff is typically located near the water meter, which in Tulsa is usually near the street at the front of your property, or in a utility room near where the main line enters the house.

If the source is a leaking appliance (washing machine, dishwasher), turn that appliance off and pull it away from the wall to access the supply line shutoff behind it.

Step 3: Turn Off Electricity in Affected Areas — If Safe

Water and electricity are a deadly combination. If water is near outlets, appliances, or the electrical panel, go to your breaker box and shut off the circuits for affected areas. If you have to walk through standing water to reach the panel, do not do it. Call us — we have the training to handle flooded areas safely.

Step 4: Document Everything

Before touching anything, take photos and videos of all affected areas. Capture standing water, saturated walls, damaged flooring, and damaged belongings. This documentation is essential for your insurance claim with State Farm, Farmers, Allstate, or any other carrier.

Most Oklahoma homeowners' insurance policies cover sudden water damage, but adjusters want to see the initial condition. Once restoration begins, the original evidence disappears.

Step 5: Move Valuables and Electronics

If it's safe to do so, move electronics, furniture, important documents, and irreplaceable items away from the water. Place aluminum foil under furniture legs to prevent staining on wet carpet. Don't move anything that could cause you to slip or fall.

Step 6: Do NOT Try to Dry It Yourself

This is where well-meaning homeowners cause additional damage. Running fans, opening windows, and using shop vacs might seem helpful but can:

  • Push moisture into unaffected wall cavities and subfloor areas
  • Draw outdoor humidity into the building during Oklahoma's humid spring months
  • Create conditions that spread mold spores through the HVAC system
  • Give your insurance adjuster reason to dispute coverage by altering the scene

Step 7: Contact Your Insurance Company

After calling us, file your claim. Most Tulsa-area insurance companies have 24/7 claim hotlines. Get a claim number. Your insurer will want to know the cause of the damage, when it occurred, and what areas are affected. We can help you communicate with your adjuster once our crew is on-site.

What Happens When We Arrive

When our Tulsa Water Damage Pros crew arrives (typically within 60 minutes), we will:

  • Assess the full scope of damage with moisture meters and thermal cameras
  • Begin truck-mounted water extraction immediately
  • Identify hidden pockets of water in walls and under flooring
  • Photograph and document damage for your insurance claim
  • Set up industrial drying equipment and begin the drying process
