Why We're Different

Three Things Homeowners Tell Us Matter Most

We Measure, We Don't Guess

Every job starts with moisture mapping using calibrated meters and infrared cameras. We don't declare a job dry because it looks dry or feels dry — we declare it dry when the readings confirm it. Our moisture logs document the full drying process, location by location, day by day. That documentation protects you with your insurance adjuster and protects your home from hidden mold.

We Handle the Insurance Work

Most homeowners have never filed a water damage claim. We have. We know what adjusters look for, how to document a loss to maximize legitimate coverage, and how to communicate with carriers on your behalf. We provide complete photographic documentation, moisture logs, material inventories, and scope of work — everything your adjuster needs to process the claim without back-and-forth delays.

We're Available When It Actually Happens

Water damage doesn't happen at 10 AM on a Tuesday. It happens at 11 PM on a Saturday during a spring storm. We answer 24/7 — a real person, not a voicemail system that calls back during business hours. We dispatch immediately on every call. The 60-minute response time is the standard, not the exception.

Our Commitment

What You Can Expect From Every Job

Every job gets a written scope of work before any work begins. If we find something during the job that wasn't visible in the initial assessment — hidden mold, structural damage, a second affected area — we stop, show you what we found, and explain what it means before any additional work proceeds. The price we quote is the price you pay for the scope we agreed on. No line items that appear after the job is done.

We provide a complete drying log with every job — moisture readings by location and date from start to clearance. This is documentation you keep. If questions arise about the restoration down the road, you have the record. That's what honest, documented work looks like.
